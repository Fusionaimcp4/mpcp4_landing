# Data Subject Rights Policy  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO) / Compliance Officer  
**Effective Date:** 2025-11-05  
**Review Cycle:** Annual or upon changes to data rights regulations  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define MCP4.ai’s framework for handling **Data Subject Requests (DSRs)** and ensuring all individuals can exercise their privacy rights under applicable laws.  
This includes rights of access, rectification, deletion, restriction, portability, objection, and consent withdrawal, in accordance with **ISO 27701**, **GDPR**, **CCPA/CPRA**, **LGPD**, and **POPIA**.

---

## 2. Scope
Applies to:  
- All MCP4.ai services and platforms — **Fusion**, **Voxe**, and **NeuroSwitch**.  
- All users, customers, employees, and partners whose personal data is processed by MCP4.ai.  
- All staff responsible for responding to data subject requests.  

This policy covers both **consumer** and **business user** data requests.

---

## 3. Key Principles
MCP4.ai processes all DSRs according to these principles:
1. **Transparency** — Users clearly informed of their rights via the Privacy Policy.  
2. **Verification** — Requests processed only after confirming the requester’s identity.  
3. **Timeliness** — All valid requests fulfilled within **30 days**, extendable by another 30 days if complex.  
4. **No Discrimination** — Users shall not face degraded service or pricing for exercising rights.  
5. **Accountability** — Each request logged, tracked, and auditable.  

---

## 4. Rights of Data Subjects
| Right | Description | Legal Basis |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **Access** | Obtain confirmation whether personal data is processed and receive a copy. | GDPR Art. 15 / CCPA §1798.100 |
| **Rectification** | Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data. | GDPR Art. 16 |
| **Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)** | Request deletion of personal data where no longer needed or processed unlawfully. | GDPR Art. 17 / LGPD Art. 18 |
| **Restriction** | Limit processing under certain conditions (e.g., dispute accuracy). | GDPR Art. 18 |
| **Portability** | Receive personal data in a structured, commonly used format and transfer to another controller. | GDPR Art. 20 |
| **Objection** | Object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing. | GDPR Art. 21 |
| **Withdraw Consent** | Withdraw previously given consent without affecting lawful processing prior to withdrawal. | GDPR Art. 7(3) |
| **Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing** | For California users, opt out of sale or sharing of personal data. | CCPA §1798.120 |
| **Automated Decision-Making Rights** | Request human intervention in decisions made solely by automated systems. | GDPR Art. 22 |

---

## 5. Submitting a Request
Users can submit DSRs through any of the following:
- **Email:** privacy@mcp4.ai  
- **Online Form:** “Privacy Request Form” available on mcp4.ai and subdomains  
- **Postal Mail:** [Insert address of MCP4.ai HQ]  

Requests must specify:
- Type of right being exercised  
- Information sufficient to verify identity  
- Details of data or systems involved (if known)

---

## 6. Identity Verification
To prevent unauthorized access:
- Users must confirm their email address and provide any necessary verification (e.g., 2FA token, account login).  
- For sensitive data requests, additional verification (photo ID or signed statement) may be required.  
- If the requester acts as an authorized agent, written authorization must be provided.

---

## 7. Request Handling Workflow
| Step | Action | Responsible | Timeframe |
|------|---------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Log request in **DSR Register** | DPO / Compliance | Immediately |
| 2 | Verify identity of requester | DPO | Within 3 business days |
| 3 | Review scope and applicable rights | Legal / Compliance | Within 5 business days |
| 4 | Collect data from relevant systems | Engineering / Data Owners | Within 15 business days |
| 5 | Prepare response and validate accuracy | DPO / Engineering | Within 25 business days |
| 6 | Deliver response securely | DPO | ≤ 30 days |
| 7 | Record completion in **DSR Register** | DPO | Upon closure |

If the request is complex or involves large datasets, the timeframe may extend by up to 30 additional days, with written notice to the requester.

---

## 8. Response Format
- Responses must be **clear, concise, and in plain language**.  
- Data provided electronically in **JSON** or **CSV** format where feasible.  
- Exclude proprietary, legal, or third-party confidential data.  
- Redact information that would compromise other users’ privacy.  

---

## 9. Fees
MCP4.ai fulfills requests free of charge unless:
- The request is **manifestly unfounded or excessive**; or  
- Repeated requests are made for the same information.  
In such cases, a reasonable administrative fee may apply.

---

## 10. Exceptions
Requests may be refused or partially fulfilled where:
- Disclosure would violate another person’s rights.  
- The data is required for legal claims or obligations.  
- It is technically infeasible to isolate specific records.  
- The request conflicts with retention obligations (e.g., financial or tax records).  

Refusals will always include the reason and information on the right to lodge a complaint.

---

## 11. Record Keeping
MCP4.ai maintains a **Data Subject Request Register** including:
- Request ID and type  
- Date received and completed  
- Verification method  
- Actions taken and outcomes  
- Responsible personnel  
- Time to resolution  

The DSR Register is reviewed quarterly for process compliance and stored securely for **3 years**.

---

## 12. Roles and Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **DPO / Compliance Officer** | Manage request intake, ensure timely and lawful response. |
| **Engineering / IT** | Locate and securely extract relevant data. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Review complex requests for exemptions or disclosure limits. |
| **Customer Support / Voxe AI** | Forward privacy-related requests promptly to DPO. |
