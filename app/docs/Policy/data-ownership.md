# Customer Data Ownership Policy  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO) / Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)  
**Effective Date:** 2025-11-05  
**Review Cycle:** Annual or after material changes to platform architecture or data handling  

---

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines MCP4.ai’s commitment to ensuring that all customers retain full ownership, control, and rights over their data stored, processed, or transmitted through MCP4.ai products — **Fusion**, **Voxe**, and **NeuroSwitch**.  
It establishes how customer data is governed, accessed, and deleted in accordance with **ISO 27018**, **ISO 27701**, **GDPR**, **CCPA**, **LGPD**, and **POPIA**.

---

## 2. Scope
Applies to:
- All customer data processed by MCP4.ai, including:  
  - User account and business data  
  - Chat transcripts, prompts, and AI interaction logs  
  - Uploaded content, files, and documents  
  - Billing and transaction metadata  
- All employees, contractors, and sub-processors with potential access to customer data.  
- All MCP4.ai services operating in cloud environments (DigitalOcean, Vercel, AWS, BTCPayServer, LNbits, Stripe, Chatwoot).  

---

## 3. Policy Statement
MCP4.ai recognizes that **customers are the sole owners of their data**.  
We act as a **data processor** on behalf of the customer, processing data only as necessary to deliver contracted services.  
MCP4.ai does not claim ownership, sell, mine, or use customer data for purposes outside the agreed scope.

---

## 4. Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|-------------|
| **Customer Data** | Any data, content, or information input, uploaded, or generated by the customer through MCP4.ai systems. |
| **Processor** | MCP4.ai when processing data on behalf of the customer. |
| **Controller** | The customer or organization that determines the purposes and means of data processing. |
| **Sub-Processor** | Third-party vendor that processes customer data under MCP4.ai’s control and Data Processing Agreement (DPA). |

---

## 5. Customer Rights
1. **Ownership:** Customers retain complete ownership of all data they input, generate, or store on MCP4.ai platforms.  
2. **Access:** Customers may access, export, or download their data at any time via the user interface or by request to **privacy@mcp4.ai**.  
3. **Control:** Customers may modify or delete their data directly through platform controls or request full deletion.  
4. **Portability:** Upon termination, customers can request a full data export in JSON or CSV format within 15 business days.  
5. **Deletion:** Upon account closure or contract end, all customer data is permanently deleted within **30 days** unless otherwise required by law.  
6. **Consent Management:** For multi-tenant environments (e.g., Voxe chat deployments), customers control consent collection for their own end users via CookieYes or integrated CMP.

---

## 6. MCP4.ai Responsibilities
MCP4.ai is responsible for:
- Processing customer data **only** per written instructions (terms of service, DPAs, or customer agreements).  
- Implementing encryption, access controls, and logging to protect customer data.  
- Ensuring sub-processors meet equivalent contractual, technical, and regulatory obligations.  
- Providing transparency regarding all locations, sub-processors, and retention periods.  
- Notifying customers within **72 hours** of any confirmed data breach affecting their data.  
- Supporting customer data-subject rights requests (access, deletion, portability, etc.) where applicable.  

---

## 7. Data Access and Use Restrictions
- MCP4.ai staff may access customer data only when strictly required for:  
  - Technical support or troubleshooting  
  - Security investigations  
  - Maintenance or system integrity checks  
- All access must be logged, time-bound, and approved by authorized management.  
- Customer data shall never be:  
  - Sold, shared, or used for marketing purposes  
  - Used to train AI models without explicit written consent  
  - Transferred outside designated data regions without contractual safeguards  

---

## 8. Sub-Processor and Vendor Obligations
All vendors and sub-processors used by MCP4.ai:
- Operate under **Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)** incorporating customer ownership clauses.  
- Must delete or return all customer data after termination.  
- Undergo regular security and privacy audits per the **Third-Party & Sub-Processor Review Policy**.  
- Are listed publicly at **https://mcp4.ai/legal/subprocessors** for transparency.  

---

## 9. Data Retention and Deletion
- Customer data retained only for as long as necessary for service delivery or legal obligations.  
- Retention periods follow the **Data Retention Policy**.  
- Upon customer request or contract termination:  
  1. All data deleted from production within **30 days**.  
  2. Backup data purged within **60 days** (rolling backup cycle).  
  3. Confirmation of deletion or anonymization provided upon completion.  

---

## 10. Transparency and Audit Rights
- Customers may request a summary of technical and organizational measures protecting their data.  
- Upon written request, customers may review audit evidence (e.g., SOC 2 / ISO 27001 certifications).  
- MCP4.ai supports external compliance reviews by regulators or customer-appointed auditors within contractual boundaries.

---

## 11. Cross-Border Data Transfers
- All transfers governed by **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** or equivalent safeguards.  
- Data hosted in regions (EU/US) aligned with customer preference.  
- Customers notified before any change to storage regions or providers.  

---

## 12. Shared Responsibility Model
| Area | Customer Responsibility | MCP4.ai Responsibility |
|------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Account Access | Secure login, password management, user permissions | Enforced MFA, session security |
| Data Input | Uploading accurate, lawful content | Encryption, segregation, storage security |
| Consent Management | Collecting user consent | Providing CMP integration (CookieYes) |
| Retention Control | Configuring retention and deletion settings | Implementing secure deletion and backup purge |
| Compliance Reporting | Providing internal compliance evidence | Supporting audits, logs, and certifications |

---

## 13. Roles and Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **DPO / CISO** | Maintain this policy, oversee customer data protection and deletion. |
| **Engineering / DevOps** | Implement encryption, backups, and deletion workflows. |
| **Legal / Compliance** | Maintain DPAs and contractual data-ownership clauses. |
| **Support / Operations** | Respond to customer data access and deletion requests. |

---

## 14. Enforcement
Violation of this policy — such as unauthorized access, export, or misuse of customer data — constitutes a **major security incident** under the **Incident Response Procedure** and may result in disciplinary action, termination, or legal consequences.

---

## 15. Review and Continuous Improvement
This policy is reviewed annually or upon:
- Introduction of new cloud infrastructure  
- Integration of new sub-processors  
- Legal or regulatory updates  

Findings from audits, incidents, and customer feedback drive continuous improvement.
