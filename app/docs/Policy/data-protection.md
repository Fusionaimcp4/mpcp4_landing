# Data Protection Policy  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO) / Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)  
**Effective Date:** 2025-11-05  
**Review Cycle:** Annual or upon legal, technical, or organizational changes  

---

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes MCP4.ai’s framework for protecting personal and confidential information processed within our systems — **Fusion**, **Voxe**, and **NeuroSwitch**.  
It defines governance, roles, and operational practices ensuring compliance with **ISO 27701**, **GDPR**, **CCPA/CPRA**, **LGPD**, **POPIA**, and **FADP**, and supports the organization’s broader Information Security Management System (ISMS).

---

## 2. Scope
Applies to:  
- All MCP4.ai entities, staff, contractors, and partners handling personal data.  
- All systems, databases, and tools that process personal or confidential data.  
- Both structured and unstructured information (digital or paper).  

Covers the **full data lifecycle**: collection → storage → use → sharing → retention → disposal.

---

## 3. Policy Objectives
1. Protect all personal data against loss, misuse, unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure.  
2. Ensure processing is lawful, transparent, and limited to stated purposes.  
3. Maintain accountability and demonstrate compliance through documentation and audit.  
4. Integrate privacy-by-design and privacy-by-default into every MCP4.ai product.  
5. Enable data subjects to exercise their rights easily and promptly.

---

## 4. Legal and Regulatory Alignment
MCP4.ai adheres to the following frameworks:
- **ISO 27701:2019** – Privacy Information Management System (PIMS).  
- **GDPR (EU 2016/679)** – Data protection principles and rights.  
- **CCPA / CPRA (California)** – Consumer transparency and opt-out rights.  
- **LGPD (Brazil)** – Consent, accountability, and cross-border rules.  
- **POPIA (South Africa)** – Lawful processing and security safeguards.  
- **FADP (Switzerland)** – Cross-border adequacy and transparency.  

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Oversees PIMS; ensures compliance; manages data subject requests; maintains breach and processing registers. |
| **CISO / Founder** | Implements technical and organizational security controls. |
| **Compliance Officer** | Monitors adherence to privacy laws and ISO 27701 clauses. |
| **Engineering Leads** | Apply privacy-by-design in systems; maintain encryption and access controls. |
| **All Employees** | Handle personal data responsibly and report incidents immediately. |

---

## 6. Data Protection Principles
All personal data must be processed in accordance with these principles:
1. **Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency** – Processing only with a lawful basis and user awareness.  
2. **Purpose Limitation** – Data collected for explicit, legitimate purposes only.  
3. **Data Minimization** – Process only what is necessary.  
4. **Accuracy** – Keep data current and correct errors quickly.  
5. **Storage Limitation** – Retain only for necessary periods.  
6. **Integrity and Confidentiality** – Secure processing via encryption, access control, and logging.  
7. **Accountability** – Demonstrate compliance through evidence and records.

---

## 7. Lawful Bases for Processing
Personal data is processed under one or more of the following bases:
- Contract performance (user accounts, subscriptions).  
- Legal obligation (invoices, taxation).  
- Legitimate interest (security, service improvement).  
- Consent (analytics, AI personalization, marketing).  

Each processing activity and legal basis are recorded in the **Data Processing Register**.

---

## 8. Privacy-by-Design and Default
- Conduct a **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** for any new product, feature, or AI model using personal data.  
- Collect the minimum data required for the intended function.  
- Default system settings favor privacy (e.g., analytics off until consent).  
- Ensure access control and encryption are applied from inception.  

---

## 9. Data Subject Rights
MCP4.ai enables individuals to exercise all applicable rights:
- Access, rectification, erasure, and portability.  
- Restriction or objection to processing.  
- Withdrawal of consent.  
- Opt-out of sale or sharing of data (CCPA/CPRA).  

Requests are logged in the **DSR Register** and fulfilled within **30 days** unless legally extended.

---

## 10. Data Retention and Disposal
- Retention periods defined in the **Data Retention Policy**.  
- Expired data automatically deleted or anonymized.  
- Secure deletion methods used for electronic media; physical records shredded.  
- Retention overrides only for legal obligations or ongoing investigations.  

---

## 11. Data Transfers
- Cross-border transfers rely on **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** or adequacy decisions.  
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).  
- Sub-processors must meet equivalent privacy and security standards.  

---

## 12. Security Controls
To protect data integrity and confidentiality:
- MFA and RBAC enforced across all platforms.  
- Audit logs retained ≥ 90 days.  
- Encryption per the **Encryption Policy**.  
- Vulnerability scans and penetration testing conducted at least annually.  
- Incident handling per the **Incident Response Procedure**.  

---

## 13. Vendor and Sub-Processor Management
- All third-party providers assessed for privacy and security compliance.  
- DPAs executed with each vendor.  
- Sub-processor list maintained and updated annually.  
- Vendor performance and compliance reviewed quarterly.  

---

## 14. Breach Notification
- Suspected or confirmed breaches reported immediately to **security@mcp4.ai**.  
- DPO assesses severity within 24 hours.  
- Notification to authorities and affected users within 72 hours where required.  
- All incidents documented in the **Data Breach Register**.  

---

## 15. Training and Awareness
- All personnel complete annual privacy and security training.  
- Developers receive privacy-by-design guidance.  
- Awareness campaigns reinforce reporting and compliance culture.

---

## 16. Documentation and Records
The following records are maintained for audit and ISO 27701 certification:
- Data Processing Register  
- DPIAs and risk assessments  
- Data Subject Request Log  
- Data Breach Register  
- Vendor/Sub-processor List  
- Consent Logs  
All records retained for **at least 3 years**.

---

## 17. Monitoring and Audit
- DPO and Compliance conduct quarterly privacy audits.  
- Findings and improvements recorded in the **Compliance Report**.  
- Independent external audits performed as needed for certification.  

---

## 18. Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, contract termination, or regulatory reporting.  
Persistent non-compliance triggers executive review and corrective measures.

---

## 19. Review and Maintenance
This policy is reviewed annually or upon significant legal, organizational, or technological changes.  
Revisions are approved by the DPO and communicated company-wide.
